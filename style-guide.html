01

Design Principles

Honor the original

Keep the Flash-era structure: fixed slide canvas, Wedge logo, T3 mark, bottom utility row, and red navigation arrows.

Make it readable

Use fewer font sizes. If text feels crowded, edit layout first instead of shrinking text into unreadability.

One system, many lessons

Every new module should feel like it belongs to the same Presenter, even when the diagram or media changes.

Standard Slide Layout

Slide Title Area

Main content / diagram / video area

©Copyright 2004-2026 The Wedge Group. All rights reserved.

Canvas: 983px by 698px original Presenter stage, scaled responsively to fit screen.

Title zone: top-left. Do not center titles unless the original slide clearly requires it.

Logo: top-right, consistent size unless the source slide requires a special placement.

Footer: fixed bottom row with legal text and about/close controls.

Navigation: red arrows near bottom-right; previous on left, next on right.

05

Buttons and Interaction States

Primary: blue, used for large app actions outside legacy slides.

Reveal / emphasis: red, used for lesson reveal actions and important controls.

Utility: small, lower-case, bottom footer only: about, close.

Hover/focus: should visibly change border, background, or shadow. Never rely on color alone for meaning.
